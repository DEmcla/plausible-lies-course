CSCI 247: Plausible Lies (Online)

6-Week Summer Session | Asynchronous | 3 Credits

Document version: 1.0 | Last updated: January 2026

Time Commitment: This is an accelerated course. Expect 14-19 hours per week. Plan your summer accordingly.

Course Overview

This course teaches you to critically evaluate AI and digital tools using a transferable framework. No coding required. You'll learn to ask the right questions about any technology—current or future.

Assessment Summary

Assessment Weight Due
AI Tool Audit 25% End of Week 3
Personal Security Audit 25% End of Week 5
Learning Portfolio 20% Checkpoints: Weeks 2, 4, 6
Tool Adaptability Exercise 30% Week 6

Weekly Structure

Each week follows the same pattern:

Day Activity
Monday New week opens: readings, videos, activity instructions posted
Monday-Thursday Complete readings and activities at your own pace
Thursday Initial discussion post due (if applicable)
Sunday 11:59pm All weekly work due: activities, discussion responses, portfolio updates

Week 1: Foundations ~16 hours

Topics: What is AI? Types of models (LLMs, image generators, classifiers). How training works conceptually. Introduction to the evaluation framework.

Readings

  • IBM: What Are Large Language Models?
  • MIT Sloan: How ChatGPT Works (video)
  • IBM: What Is AI Bias?

Activities

  • Model Matching Exercise: Match 5 tasks to appropriate AI model types
  • Training Data Detective: Investigate an AI tool's training data documentation
  • Portfolio Setup: Create Word document with cover page, TOC, section headings
  • Discussion: Introduce yourself + share one AI tool you use regularly

Due Sunday: All activities, discussion post + 2 responses, portfolio structure

Week 2: Testing Claims ~17 hours

Topics: Designing controlled comparisons. Documenting methodology. Why reproducibility matters.

Readings

  • AIMultiple: Reproducible AI
  • Calling Bullshit: Selected syllabus modules
  • ACM Queue: GPTs and Hallucination (optional, for deeper understanding)

Activities

  • Reproducibility Test: Same prompt to Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini—document results
  • Prompt Sensitivity: Same question, three phrasings, one model—document changes
  • Discussion: Share surprising finding from comparison exercises
Portfolio Checkpoint 1: Cover page, working TOC, all section headings. At least one artifact started.

Due Sunday: All activities, discussion + responses, Portfolio Checkpoint 1

Week 3: Evaluation Framework Deep Dive ~18 hours

Topics: Applying the full framework (Function, Data, Failures, Access, Terms, Impact). Structured tool evaluation.

Readings

  • Calling Bullshit: Case studies (selected)
  • Review: Evaluation framework components

Activities

  • AI Tool Audit (Major Assessment): This week's primary work is your AI Tool Audit—a full evaluation of an unfamiliar AI tool using all six framework components. See the AI Tool Audit assignment for details.
  • Discussion: What was hardest to find information about your tool? Why do you think that information was difficult to locate?
AI Tool Audit Due: Full evaluation of an AI tool using the framework.
This is a major assessment (25%).

Due Sunday: AI Tool Audit, discussion + responses

Week 4: Visualizations & Data ~17 hours

Topics: How AI generates graphs. Spotting misleading visualizations. Evaluating benchmark claims.

Readings

  • ThoughtSpot: How to Identify Misleading Graphs
  • Nature: Is your AI benchmark lying to you?
  • ACM CHI: Misleading Beyond Visual Tricks (optional)

Activities

  • Graph or Garbage: Have AI generate a chart, evaluate its validity
  • Benchmark BS Detection: Find a published AI benchmark, identify potential issues
  • Make It Lie: Use AI to create two visualizations supporting opposite conclusions from same data
  • Discussion: Share most misleading visualization you found
Portfolio Checkpoint 2: At least 3 artifacts with reflections. Table of figures if using images. Consistent formatting throughout.

Due Sunday: All activities, discussion + responses, Portfolio Checkpoint 2

Week 5: Communication & Synthesis ~16 hours

Topics: Writing for different audiences. Methodology documentation. Professional communication.

Readings

  • Document360: Technical Writing for Non-Technical Audiences
  • USC Libraries: Writing Methodology Sections

Activities

  • Critique the Explanation: Evaluate an AI-generated explanation, identify issues, propose fixes
  • Methodology Audit: Review a classmate's comparison exercise—could you reproduce it?
  • Discussion: What makes technical writing clear vs. confusing?
Personal Security Audit Due: Audit your digital footprint and implement real changes.
This is a major assessment (25%).

Due Sunday: Personal Security Audit, activities, discussion + responses

Week 6: Application ~18 hours

Topics: Applying all skills to a novel tool. Demonstrating framework mastery.

Activities

  • Tool Adaptability Exercise: Evaluate a novel AI tool under time pressure, demonstrating all course skills
  • Synthesis Reflection: Complete final portfolio reflection
Tool Adaptability Exercise: Final assessment (30%). Novel tool revealed Monday. Full evaluation due by end of week.
Portfolio Checkpoint 3 (Final): All 6 artifacts, all reflections, synthesis reflection. Complete, polished document.

Due Sunday: Tool Adaptability Exercise, Final Portfolio

Time Management Tips

  • Don't fall behind. In a 6-week course, missing one week puts you 17% behind. There's no catch-up time built in.
  • Start the portfolio early. Week 1's portfolio setup saves you from learning Word skills under deadline pressure later.
  • Spread work across the week. Don't save everything for Sunday. Aim to complete readings by Wednesday, activities by Friday.
  • Major assessments overlap with weekly work. Weeks 3, 5, and 6 are heavy. Plan ahead.

Late Work Policy

Weekly activities: 10% penalty per day late, up to 3 days. After 3 days, no credit.

Major assessments: 10% penalty per day late, up to 5 days. After 5 days, no credit.

Portfolio checkpoints: Must be submitted to receive feedback. Late checkpoints receive reduced feedback.
