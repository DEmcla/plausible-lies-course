Learning Portfolio

Throughout the semester, you'll build a single Word document that grows into your learning portfolio. This isn't something you compile at the end—it's a document you develop week by week, adding artifacts and reflections as you complete them.

The portfolio serves two purposes: it demonstrates your growth in evaluating technology, and it develops your professional document skills in Word.

The Document You're Building

Your portfolio is one Word document (.docx) that includes:

  • Cover page — Title, your name, course, date
  • Table of contents — Dynamic, auto-generated from headings
  • Six artifact sections — One for each learning outcome, each containing:
    • The artifact itself (your work)
    • Your reflection on that artifact
  • Synthesis reflection — Final section, added at end of semester
  • Table of figures — If you include images, charts, or screenshots

Word Skills You'll Develop

Building this document teaches you professional Word skills:

  • Heading styles — Required for the table of contents to work
  • Dynamic table of contents — Updates automatically as you add content
  • Section breaks — Different formatting for different parts
  • Cover page — Professional document design
  • Figure captions — Proper labeling for images and charts
  • Table of figures — Auto-generated from captions
  • Cross-references — "See Figure 3" that updates if numbering changes
  • Consistent formatting — Using styles instead of manual formatting

These are skills you'll use in any professional or academic context.

Submission Schedule

You'll submit your portfolio document multiple times throughout the semester. Each submission builds on the previous one.

Checkpoint When What's Expected
1: Structure End of Week 3 Cover page, table of contents, section headings for all six outcomes (empty sections are fine). Document structure is in place.
2: First Artifacts End of Week 6 At least two artifacts with reflections added. Table of contents updates correctly.
3: Midpoint End of Week 9 At least four artifacts with reflections. Table of figures if applicable. Document formatting is consistent.
4: Final End of Week 14 All six artifacts, all reflections, synthesis reflection. Complete, polished document.

The Six Artifacts

Select one piece of work demonstrating each learning outcome:

Outcome Example Artifacts
Explain how AI models work and select appropriate tools Model matching exercise, training data investigation
Apply the evaluation framework Framework application from any unit
Communicate findings clearly Findings paper, methodology audit, critique
Interpret and critique data visualizations Graph evaluation, benchmark analysis, "Make It Lie" exercise
Design and conduct controlled comparisons Reproducibility test, prompt sensitivity exercise
Evaluate source reliability Training data detective, any source evaluation work

You choose which artifacts best represent your abilities. You may revise earlier work before including it.

Reflections

Artifact Reflections

For each artifact, write 1-2 paragraphs addressing:

  • Why does this piece demonstrate the outcome?
  • What would you improve if you did it again?
  • What did you learn from the process?

Be specific. "I learned a lot" says nothing. "I realized I was trusting AI-generated citations without checking them" says something.

Synthesis Reflection

Added at the end of the semester. One page addressing:

  • How has your thinking about AI and technology evaluation changed?
  • Which skill was hardest for you to develop? Why?
  • If you encountered a completely new AI tool tomorrow, how would you approach evaluating it?

How It's Graded

Checkpoints 1-3 (During Semester)

Graded on completion and document structure, not content quality:

  • Did you submit on time?
  • Does your table of contents work?
  • Are you using heading styles correctly?
  • Is formatting consistent?

You'll receive feedback on Word skills and structure. This is formative—helping you improve, not penalizing you.

Checkpoint 4: Final Portfolio

Criterion Weight What I'm Looking For
Artifacts 30% All six outcomes represented; artifacts actually demonstrate the claimed outcome
Reflections 25% Honest self-assessment; specific observations, not generic praise
Synthesis 20% Evidence of genuine growth; clear articulation of your evaluation approach
Document Quality 25% Professional formatting; working TOC and table of figures; consistent styles; polished presentation

Getting Started

In Week 1, you'll create your portfolio document with:

  1. A cover page
  2. A table of contents (Insert → Table of Contents)
  3. Six sections with Heading 1 styles, one for each outcome
  4. A final section for your synthesis reflection

The document will be mostly empty—that's expected. You're building the structure first. Content comes as you complete activities throughout the semester.

Don't wait until the end. Students who try to build this document in Week 14 struggle with the Word skills because they didn't practice incrementally. The checkpoint submissions exist to keep you on track.

A Note on Revision

You may revise any artifact before including it in your portfolio. That's the point—learning means improving. Just don't fabricate work you didn't do.
