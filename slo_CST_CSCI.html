Institutional Learning Outcomes Reference

This document contains the Institutional, Tier II, and Department-level outcomes for Computer Science courses at MCLA. Use this as a reference when creating course-specific syllabus outcomes documents.

MCLA Institutional Learning Outcomes

All MCLA graduates demonstrate proficiency in these three overarching goals:

# Outcome Description
1 Effective Communication Utilize multiple forms of expression to participate in our global community; choose appropriate communication forms for diverse audiences and defend positions referencing multiple perspectives
2 Analytical Inquiry Engage in analytical inquiry to address complex problems; synthesize contrasting information, apply contextually appropriate methods, and connect across disciplines
3 Active & Ethical Engagement Be active, engaged, and ethical individuals; engage in meaningful self-reflection and evaluate own learning and contributions

Tier II: Science & Technology Outcomes

MCLA's Tier II Science & Technology requirement develops scientific literacy. Courses fulfilling this requirement address:

# Outcome Description
1 Content Knowledge Demonstrate knowledge of natural science content
2 Problem Solving Apply content knowledge to solve problems
3 Communication Communicate scientific findings
4 Graphs & Visualization Interpret and generate graphs
5 Scientific Practices Apply scientific practices
6 Source Evaluation Evaluate source reliability

MCLA Computer Science Department Outcomes

To graduate with an MCLA CS Degree, students must demonstrate proficiency in:

# Outcome Description
1 Fundamentals Develop foundational computer science skills
2 Design, Build, Test, Maintain Design, build, test, and maintain professional quality software applications, information technology solutions, and business information systems
3 Analysis & Evaluation Apply knowledge of important social, ethical, and professional considerations related to their specific computer science concentration when analyzing new situations and solving real-world problems
4 Communication & Teamwork Communicate ideas effectively with others, working both independently and within a development team environment
5 Life-long Learning Learn about new ideas and advances, techniques, tools, and languages within their specific concentration and use them effectively, and to be motivated to engage in life-long learning

Usage

When creating a course syllabus outcomes document:

  1. Copy the relevant sections above
  2. Define course-specific Student Learning Outcomes (SLOs)
  3. Map course SLOs to both Tier II and Department outcomes
  4. Map assessments to course SLOs
