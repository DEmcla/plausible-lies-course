Grading Rubrics

Document version: 1.0 | Last updated: January 2026

Grading Scale

  • Excellent (90-100%): Exceeds expectations. Demonstrates mastery.
  • Proficient (75-89%): Meets expectations. Solid work with minor gaps.
  • Developing (60-74%): Approaching expectations. Significant gaps but shows understanding.
  • Incomplete (0-59%): Does not meet expectations. Major components missing or incorrect.

AI Privacy & Security Audit (25%)

Audit your AI tool usage, evaluate privacy policies using the framework, and make informed decisions about your data.

Tier II Outcomes Assessed: Problem Solving, Source Evaluation, Communication
Criterion Weight Excellent Proficient Developing Incomplete
AI Tool Inventory
Problem Solving		 20% Comprehensive inventory of AI tools used. Examines what's been shared, account security, available settings. Discovers non-obvious data exposure. Reviews major AI tools and what's been shared. Reasonable coverage of settings. Limited inventory. Covers obvious tools only. Doesn't examine what's been shared. Superficial listing. No examination of actual usage or data shared.
Framework Analysis
Source Evaluation		 25% Applies framework to analyze actual privacy policies. Cites specific terms. Identifies concerning clauses. Compares across tools. Analyzes privacy policies with some specific citations. Identifies main privacy concerns. General statements about privacy without reading actual policies. Assumptions rather than analysis. No evidence of reading or analyzing privacy policies.
Risk Assessment
Problem Solving		 20% Realistic assessment of personal risks. Distinguishes between theoretical and practical concerns. Makes reasoned decisions about trade-offs. Identifies main risks with reasonable assessment. Some trade-off analysis. Risks identified but assessment unrealistic (paranoid or dismissive). No trade-off reasoning. Few risks identified. No meaningful assessment.
Actions Taken
Problem Solving		 20% Implements meaningful changes with evidence. Actions reflect analysis. Explains reasoning for what was changed and what was accepted. Implements several changes with some evidence. Most actions relate to analysis. Minimal changes. Limited evidence. Unclear connection to analysis. No meaningful changes implemented or documented.
Reflection & Documentation
Communication		 15% Honest reflection on trade-offs. Clear, organized presentation. Acknowledges accepted risks with reasoning. Reasonable reflection. Generally clear documentation. Superficial reflection. Documentation unclear. Little reflection. Process cannot be understood.

Tier II Outcomes Assessed: All six outcomes through artifacts; Communication through document quality

Weekly Activities (Spot-Checked)

Weekly activities contribute to the Portfolio grade and are spot-checked for completion. Not individually graded in detail, but should meet these standards:

Standard Meets Standard Does Not Meet Standard
Completeness All required sections addressed Sections missing or clearly skipped
Effort Genuine engagement with the activity Minimal effort, placeholder responses
Timeliness Submitted by deadline Late or missing

Purpose of spot-checking: To catch students falling behind early and provide feedback, not to grade every detail. Quality of weekly work is ultimately assessed through the portfolio.
