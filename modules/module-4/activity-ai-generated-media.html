AI-Generated Media & Deepfakes

Purpose: Understand how AI generates images, audio, and video; learn to identify synthetic media; and consider the ethical implications of these technologies.

Time: 60-75 minutes

The New Reality of Synthetic Media

AI can now generate increasingly convincing:

  • Images: Photorealistic faces, scenes, artwork (DALL-E, Midjourney, Stable Diffusion)
  • Audio: Voice cloning, music, sound effects (ElevenLabs, various tools)
  • Video: Face swaps, lip syncing, full video generation (deepfake tools, Sora, Runway)

This has profound implications for evidence, trust, and verification.

How AI Image Generation Works

AI image generators don't "find" images—they create them from scratch based on patterns learned from millions of training images.

  • Text-to-image: Describe what you want; AI generates it
  • Image-to-image: Modify existing images based on prompts
  • Inpainting: Add or remove elements from existing images

The AI has no concept of "truth"—it generates whatever matches the statistical patterns for your prompt.

How Deepfakes Work

"Deepfake" typically refers to AI-generated videos that swap faces or manipulate someone's appearance/voice to make them appear to say or do things they never did.

  • Face swap: Replace one person's face with another
  • Lip sync: Make someone appear to speak different words
  • Voice cloning: Generate speech in someone's voice from text
  • Full synthesis: Generate entirely fictional video content

Detection: What to Look For

Important Caveat

Detection is an arms race. Today's tells may not work tomorrow. The goal isn't to become a perfect detector—it's to develop healthy skepticism and verification habits.

Common Artifacts in AI Images

Element What to Check
Hands & Fingers Wrong number of fingers, impossible positions, melted appearance
Text Garbled letters, nonsense words, inconsistent fonts
Backgrounds Objects that blend together, impossible architecture, repeating patterns
Symmetry Earrings, glasses, or facial features that don't match left/right
Edges Blurry boundaries between objects, hair that merges with background
Context Shadows going wrong directions, reflections that don't match

Common Artifacts in AI Video/Audio

Element What to Check
Lip sync Mouth movements slightly off from audio
Blinking Unnatural blink patterns (too little or too regular)
Skin texture Too smooth, inconsistent between frames
Voice Unusual cadence, lack of breathing sounds, consistent volume
Lighting Inconsistent shadows frame-to-frame

Ethical Dimensions

The Harm Landscape

AI-generated media creates real harms:

  • Non-consensual imagery: Creating fake intimate images of real people
  • Fraud: Voice cloning for phone scams targeting families
  • Disinformation: Fake videos of politicians or events
  • Evidence manipulation: Undermining trust in legitimate recordings
  • Harassment: Creating fake content to damage reputations

The "Liar's Dividend"

A particularly insidious effect: As synthetic media becomes common, people can dismiss real evidence as fake. This benefits bad actors—they can claim authentic recordings are AI-generated.

  2. An artist uses AI to generate images "in the style of" a famous living artist. Is this ethical? Legal?
  3. A company uses AI voice cloning to have a deceased celebrity "endorse" their product. What are the implications?

Write 200-300 words analyzing the ethical issues and what policies or practices might address them.

Key Takeaways

  • Verification is essential: Visual evidence can no longer be trusted at face value
  • Context matters: Where did this media come from? Who benefits from it being believed?
  • Detection is temporary: AI improves faster than detection methods
  • The real defense is process: Verify sources, corroborate claims, maintain healthy skepticism
