AI Search vs Traditional Research

Purpose: Understand when AI-powered search adds value vs. when traditional research methods are more reliable. Develop citation verification skills essential for academic and professional work.

Time: 60-75 minutes

The New Research Landscape

AI tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google's AI Overviews have changed how people search for information. But these tools work fundamentally differently from traditional search engines and library databases.

How They Differ

When AI Search Works Well

  • Concept explanation: "Explain the difference between correlation and causation"
  • Brainstorming: "What are some approaches to reducing food waste?"
  • Summarizing known topics: "Summarize the main arguments for and against remote work"
  • Getting started: Finding terminology and concepts to search for in proper databases

When Traditional Research Is Essential

  • Citing specific sources: Academic papers, legal cases, statistics
  • Current events: Recent news, data, or developments
  • Controversial topics: Where accuracy and source credibility matter
  • Professional/legal contexts: Medical information, legal precedent, financial data
  • Anything requiring verification: Facts that will be quoted or acted upon

The Citation Problem

AI tools often provide citations that look legitimate but are partially or completely fabricated:

  • Real author name + fake paper title
  • Real journal + fake article
  • Plausible-sounding but nonexistent sources
  • Real sources with fabricated quotes or statistics

Every citation from an AI tool must be verified before use.

Exercise: Citation Verification

Part 1: Generate Citations

Choose a topic relevant to your interests or major. Ask an AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, or Perplexity):

"Give me 5 academic sources with full citations (author, title, journal, year) about [your topic]. Include sources from the last 10 years."

Record the citations exactly as provided.

Part 2: Verify Each Citation

For each citation, attempt to verify:

  1. Does the source exist? Search Google Scholar, your library database, or the journal's website
  2. Are the details correct? Check author names, title, year, journal name
  3. Can you access it? Find the actual document to confirm it says what the AI claimed

Create a verification table:

Citation Exists? Details Accurate? Notes
[Citation 1] Yes/No/Partial Yes/No/Partial [What was wrong?]

Part 3: Compare with Database Search

Now search for sources on the same topic using:

  • Google Scholar
  • Your library's academic databases

Find 3 real sources. Note how the experience differs from the AI search.

Key Insight: AI as Starting Point, Not Endpoint

AI search can help you:

  • Identify relevant terminology and concepts
  • Get an overview of a topic before diving into primary sources
  • Generate questions to investigate through proper research

AI search should never be:

  • Your only source for citations
  • Trusted without verification
  • Used for final academic or professional work without checking original sources
